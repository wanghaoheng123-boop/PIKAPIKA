# 3D assets and intellectual property

## Commercial rule

- **Do not** ship characters, models, names, or distinctive likenesses from third-party franchises (for example Pokémon / Pikachu, Disney characters, etc.) unless you have a **written license**.
- The in-app **“Spark”** preset is an **original** simplified mascot silhouette (geometry authored in code). Marketing may describe it as “electric-mascot” or “Pokémon-inspired vibe” but must **not** claim or imply it is Pikachu or any Nintendo property.

## Bundled sample USDZ (PIKAPIKA)

- Under **`Apps/PIKAPIKA/PIKAPIKA/SamplePets/`** the app ships three **tiny demonstration** models (`PikaSampleCat`, `PikaSampleDog`, `PikaSampleSpark`). They are **original low-poly tetrahedron-style shapes** (color only suggests cat/dog/spark; they are not scans of real animals or third-party characters).
- They are generated by **`Scripts/generate-sample-usdz.py`** (ZIP + root USDA per USDZ packaging). Re-run that script after changing the mesh; commit the updated `.usdz` binaries.
- **Procedural presets** (`cat`, `dog`, `spark` in SceneKit code) still apply when **no** USDZ path is set; samples exist so users can confirm **real USDZ → SceneKit** import behavior before bringing their own files.

## Other bundled USDZ (future)

- Any additional `.usdz` shipped for marketing or realism needs **clear provenance** (license text, author, date) in this document or `NOTICE` before App Store submission.

## User-generated content

- Imported USDZ and photos are **user responsibility**; the app should not redistribute them without consent and privacy policy alignment.
